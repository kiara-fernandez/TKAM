To Kill a Mockingbird

The novel takes place in Maycomb Alabama in the 1930's where Negroes were not slaves but they were not treated at equals either. As the Great Depression worsened, so did the maltreatement of the black community. Tom Robinson, a black man, fell victim to racial prejudice even after Atticus Finch, a lawyer defended him justly in court. Jean Louise "Scout" Finch learns how hypocresy, prejudice and racism can ruin an innocent person's life and that not everything society deems correct is fair nor should it be accepted.

As Scout grows older, she realizes that sometimes, being different is the right way to be. Even if society disagrees.

Dare to defy society even if it's for the wellbeing of one innocent person.
